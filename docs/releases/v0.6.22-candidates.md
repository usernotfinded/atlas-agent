## Released

- **CAND-016: Release-Maintenance Drift Hardening** — released on 2026-07-13 as
  part of `v0.6.22`.
  - Hardens the release-maintenance path so release-identity state stays
    metadata-driven instead of drifting behind hardcoded version literals.
  - Adds a reverse-drift roadmap guard to
    `scripts/check_public_docs_consistency.py`
    (`_released_candidate_ids` and
    `_check_autonomy_roadmap_released_candidates_not_next_planned`) that flags an
    already-released candidate listed as a candidate-entry bullet under the
    next-planned planning line, plus deterministic tests; retains the
    metadata-driven release-assurance demo default.
  - Docs/checker/test-only: no runtime, safety, CLI, broker, provider, credential,
    version, or release-metadata behavior changes; does not broaden the CAND-014
    boundary.
  - Directly addresses the drift observed after the `v0.6.21` cutover (stale
    next-planned tag guard, stale demo default, roadmap candidate mislabeling).
  - Design and plan: [docs/cand-016-release-maintenance-drift-hardening.md](../cand-016-release-maintenance-drift-hardening.md)
  - The supporting guard and tests have landed on `main` as unreleased supporting
    work and are now released in `v0.6.22`.
