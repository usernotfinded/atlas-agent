## Release-maintenance hardening completed

The `v0.6.21` cutover and its stabilization surfaced release-maintenance drift
caused by hardcoded release literals. These are now metadata-driven:

- **Next-planned tag guard** in `check_bounded_autonomy_governance.py`,
  `check_autonomous_paper_workflow_demo.py`, and
  `check_paper_provider_isolation.py` queries `NEXT_PLANNED_TAG` from release
  metadata, with regression coverage in `tests/test_next_planned_tag_guard.py`.
- **Release-assurance demo default** in
  `scripts/demo_release_assurance_snapshot_bundle.sh` derives the assured release
  from `current_public_release` (pinned fallback), resolving the post-release
  full-suite CI failure.
- **Reverse-drift roadmap guard** in `check_public_docs_consistency.py` flags an
  already-released candidate listed under the next-planned planning line, with
  deterministic tests. This is the CAND-016 deliverable.

Deliberate per-release version-pin tripwire assertions (e.g.
`if CURRENT_PUBLIC_TAG != "v0.6.21"`) are intentionally left as-is so they still
fail at cutover to force a review.
